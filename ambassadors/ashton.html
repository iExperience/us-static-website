Bio

Ashton is a Sophomore at Yale and an Electrical Engineering and Economics double major. He is interested in the intersection of engineering and entrepreneurship. Ashton has always been a proponent of learning how to code thinks iExperience helps provide the background required to realize a tech vision. Ashton also write for the Yale Daily News and is working on building Yale’s first Mars Rover for the University Rover Challenge in the Spring.

Why I think coding is important

"Coding is one of the very few skills that you can use to take an abstract thought and create a working model of that idea in a short period of time without much cost or equipment. Learning to code enables people to realize their visions and solve a plethora of problems with little overhead. You can improve the quality of life and connect to more people almost instantly thanks to the universal nature of software."

Why I'm excited for iExperience

"I am excited about iExperience because it will have two big impacts in my eyes: first, it will open up students from top universities to opportunities they would not have had without coding experience; second, it will show intelligent people a new and exciting part of the world with a budding startup culture. There’s nothing like being immersed in a startup atmosphere, especially when the entire industry is growing in a new market."
