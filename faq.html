“I think everybody in this country should learn how to program a computer because it teaches you how to think.” - Steve Jobs

Why is it called iExperience?

We want you to have an experience, not just spend the whole time coding. Our program combines all the beautiful experiences of living in Cape Town while providing you with the skills to be a web developer.

Do I need to know how to program?

No. All you need is passion and the ability to unlearn the word "quit."

If I already know how to program a little, is iExperience for me?

Absolutely. iExerience is more than just learning how to program – it's learning how to be a coder. It's learning how to build your own prototypes. It's learning where to go to find answers. It's learning about what the atmosphere of working in a startup is really like. Its about experiencing technology in a new culture.

What is the structure of the program over 8 weeks?

Phase 1 (weeks 1 and 2): How the web works, MVC, and front end development, HTML CSS and Javascript.

Phase 2 (week 3): Basic Ruby, how to think and communicate like a programmer, Database and ActiveRecord.

Phase 3 (week 4): Putting these all together through the Rails framework and building an original APP from scratch!

Phase 4 (weeks 4 to 8): Spending time applying your skills in a real life work environment and learning from other developers during your internship.

What kind of students are you looking for?

We want people who want to work for tech companies and/or start their own business. If you've written code on the side or tinkered with a website, come talk to us. If you're a designer who wants to learn how to code, please apply.

When I hear the word bootcamp I think of the army. Will there be pushups?

Not likely, but there will definitely be yoga, stretching, surfing lessons, mountain biking and volleyball on the beach. Sitting in front of a computer all day can be tough on the body and mind, and we will be teaching you good habits and ways to take care of yourself. Plus, at the end of the day, what we really care about is helping great people learn into themselves. Coding is our main vehicle, but it's not our only one.

Why Ruby on Rails?

We focus on Rails for many reasons. The easiest to discover is the demand for Rail developers in the job market. But why is there such high demand for Rails developers? It's because startups love Rails for how quickly you can express your product ideas with it. It's ideal for time-to-market sensitive projects. The software developer community embraced Rails because it removes a lot of the mundane issues of web development.

Can I really learn to program in 8 weeks?

Absolutely. Programming expertise takes years to master, but that's not the point of iExperience. We want to give you a foundation so that you can be a top candidate for a future internship at a world class startup or technology company. After the classroom training and hands on internship you will have the enough skill to add tremendous value to any tech team. To learn more about the underlying research, see this essay on Why is Learning to Code So Hard
